Case Study

Narrative Puzzle Factory

A 2D top-down narrative puzzle prototype where player choices affect both puzzle outcomes and relationships with sentient robots.

Back to Projects
Role: Game Designer / Systems Designer / Unity Developer Engine: Unity Focus: Narrative + Puzzle Design Status: Prototype

Overview

Project Summary

This project explores how narrative choice can be embedded directly into puzzle solving rather than existing only in dialogue or cutscenes.

The player investigates a robot factory and interacts with robot NPCs through different approaches, including talking, hacking, or destroying. These choices change both the mechanical path through puzzles and the player’s relationship to the world.

Design Problem

What was I trying to solve?

I wanted to create a puzzle game where the player’s method of solving a problem carried narrative meaning. Instead of puzzle interaction being emotionally neutral, I wanted choices to communicate character and values.

Design Goals

Goals and constraints

Make the player’s verbs meaningful

The player should not just “solve the puzzle,” but reveal something about how they approach other beings and systems.

Keep puzzle logic readable

Even with narrative complexity, the player still needs clear feedback, understandable interactions, and readable level structure.

Support multiple solution styles

The game should reward different approaches while still making those differences feel intentional and consequential.

My Contribution

What I designed

  • Designed the core interaction model around Talk, Hack, and Destroy
  • Built prototype puzzle scenarios in Unity
  • Explored how robot NPC behavior could reinforce tone and consequence
  • Worked on gameplay readability, progression, and player feedback

Iteration

What changed during development?

Early version

Initial puzzle interactions were functional, but the emotional difference between choices was not always clear to the player.

Revision

I adjusted how NPCs communicated information and how puzzle paths were framed, so that non-destructive and destructive approaches felt more distinct.

Result

The prototype better supported the intended tension between efficient problem-solving and empathetic engagement.

Takeaway

What I learned

This project reinforced how strongly mechanics can carry narrative meaning when player verbs are tied directly to tone and consequence.

It also helped me think more clearly about readable level design, player feedback, and how to communicate systemic differences without overwhelming the player with text.
